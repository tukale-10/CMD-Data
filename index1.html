01

Where the Company stands today

An honest look at the balance sheet as at 7 April 2026.

The Company is profitable in design and proven in the market, but its most recent balance sheet shows a hard truth: it is asset-rich but cash-poor. Almost all of its value is locked in stock and amounts owed by customers, leaving very little liquid cash to fund the next import cycle. This is the core reason continued support is needed now.

Asset composition

Of more than ETB 15M in total assets, only about ETB 1M, just over 6%, is liquid cash. The rest is tied up in inventory waiting to sell and receivables waiting to be collected.

The liquidity squeeze

Cash on hand has fallen sharply from its year-one peak, even as the business itself stayed sound.

02

We are currently in a loss

After a profitable first year, the Company has since slipped into a loss position. This is stated plainly, because it is exactly why support is needed, and because the cause is not failure.
●
Capital is trapped, not lost
More than ETB 10M sits in inventory. That stock has real value and converts to cash as it sells, but while it sits, it cannot fund the next import cycle.
●
Too few import cycles
Running only about two cycles a year means long stretches where money is locked in waiting stock, with thin cash to bridge the gap.
●
The learning phase cost cash
Importing slow-moving lines such as test kits and multivitamins, and one-off setup costs, drew down the cash cushion a young company needs.
The Company is not in difficulty because the model does not work. It is in difficulty because it has been running a proven model with too little working capital, and too few cycles, to keep cash flowing freely. That is a solvable problem, and solving it is exactly what continued support would do.
